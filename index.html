About Me

Hi, I'm Shivakumar V, a PhD scholar in Product Design and Engineering at the Department of Design and Manufacturing (DnM, formerly CPDM) at IISc Bangalore. My personal experience as a prosthetic hand user fuels my commitment to developing smarter, more accessible prosthetics. In this space, I share my journey and insights into the cutting-edge innovations at the intersection of Smart Prosthetics, VLSI, and Intent Recognition. My mission is to bridge the gap between human intent and technology, crafting solutions that enhance everyday life while being affordable for underprivileged communities.

Academic Projects

  • Solar Piezo Hybrid Power Charging System: The aim of this project was to harness renewable energy sources for smart battery charging. By integrating solar panels and piezoelectric materials that generate power from footstep pressure, I developed a hybrid charging system that promotes sustainability.
  • Analysis of Power Dissipations for Circuits Using Multi-Voltage Techniques: In my M.Tech mini project, I investigated multilevel voltage scaling techniques, applying them to reduce overall power consumption in circuit designs.
  • Design and Validation of AI-IPs on FPGA: This project focused on implementing Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) on FPGA for real-time image recognition and classification.
  • Design and Verification of 32-Bit Pipelined RISC Architecture: This project revolved around designing an efficient 32-bit RISC processor using Verilog modeling and UVM testbench methodology.

Publications
